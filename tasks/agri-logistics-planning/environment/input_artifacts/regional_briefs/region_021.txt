REGIONAL MISSION BRIEF: region_021

REGION: Tigray

ZONE: Southern

The socio-economic landscape in Southern (Tigray) remains influenced by fluctuating commodity prices and shifting labor dynamics. Seasonal migration for the coffee harvest in neighboring zones has temporarily reduced available farm labor in local cooperatives.

Climatological models for Southern predict a near-average Meher season, with rainfall expected to peak in early August. However, soil moisture sensors in the northern uplands of Tigray indicate a potential deficit if current dry spells persist.

Technical assessments of Wheat yield in Southern suggest an average productivity of 2.8 tons per hectare, which is slightly above the five-year mean for the Tigray region. Maize performance, however, remains vulnerable to Striga infestation.

Fuel availability in Southern has been stabilized by the national strategic reserve, but local distribution lead times have increased by 15% due to checkpoint congestion on the main artery into Tigray.
