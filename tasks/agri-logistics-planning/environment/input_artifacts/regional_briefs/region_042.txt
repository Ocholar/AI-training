REGIONAL MISSION BRIEF: region_042

REGION: South West Ethiopia

ZONE: Keffa

------------------------------

The socio-economic landscape in Keffa (South West Ethiopia) remains influenced by fluctuating commodity prices and shifting labor dynamics. Seasonal migration for the coffee harvest in neighboring zones has temporarily reduced available farm labor in local cooperatives.

Climatological models for Keffa predict a near-average Meher season, with rainfall expected to peak in early August. However, soil moisture sensors in the northern uplands of South West Ethiopia indicate a potential deficit if current dry spells persist.

Technical assessments of Wheat yield in Keffa suggest an average productivity of 2.8 tons per hectare, which is slightly above the five-year mean for the South West Ethiopia region. Maize performance, however, remains vulnerable to Striga infestation.

Warehouse inspections in Keffa reveal mixed integrity levels. Hub A-12 remains operational at 90% capacity, while secondary storage units in South West Ethiopia require immediate roof repairs before the rainy season commences.
